Hi, There



Iam Huascar (pronounced wäs-kär), a CS grad student working with Jim Whitehead, and part of SIL.

My core research area is software engineering, with a focus on search-driven development, software evolution, and on the human aspects of software development.

The goal of my research is to improve programmers' productivity. To achieve this, I study the above areas with the aim of inventing technologies that help people build applications opportunistically by iteratively finding, combining, and reusing online source code.

I am also interested in the development and application of computational methods to address serious social issues in today's world, such as bullying in social networks.

Previous and Current Collaborations

I have done research with Neoklis Polyzotis and Yi Zhang, in the areas of automatic index tuning and bullying detection in social networks respectively.
