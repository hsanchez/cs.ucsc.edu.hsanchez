Hi there, I'm Huascar (pronounced wäs-kär), a CS grad student working with Prof. Jim Whitehead, and part of SIL.

My core research area is software engineering, with a focus on search-driven software development, software evolution, and on the human aspects of software development. The goal of my research is to improve programmer productivity. To achieve this, I study the above areas with the aim of inventing technologies that help people create, understand, and modify software rapidly.

I am also interested in the development and application of computational methods to address serious social issues in today's society, such as bullying in social networks.


previous and current collaborations

I have done research with professors Neoklis (Alkis) Polyzotis and Yi Zhang, in the areas of automatic index tuning and cyber-bullying detection respectively.
